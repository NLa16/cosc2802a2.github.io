A day in the life of a Game Developer

Being a game developer has always been one of my goals to start my own career before I arrive at RMIT. However, I do wonder what exactly would a day of a game developer would be like, and the answer, although there are various answers even just by clicking on Youtube videos, it gave me an insight on how a day in the life of a game developer (whether they are doing a full-time job, a student in-training, or a hobbyist developer) would feel like.

Through these videos, at first we would think that a game developer has a very easy life. It turns out that they need to have very good time management skills in order to have a simple life like that. A student or hobbyist game developer would spend their morning checking for feedback from testing users, working and updating on their games before they go to school/work. A full time worker will most likely go straight to work after they prepare for their morning, and spend most of their morning reading update mails, and coding. A student/hobbyist developer will definitely have more personal time for themselves so they can hang out in public places in the gym to improve their health. A full time developer will spend most of their time at work with game development as their main responsibility, so when they get off work they can hang out with their co-workers before the day ends.
